Western Cape Province

Home to iconic Table Mountain, world-class winelands, and breathtaking coastal scenery

About Western Cape

The Western Cape is a province of South Africa, situated on the southwestern coast of the country. It is the fourth-largest of the nine provinces with an area of 129,449 square kilometres, and the third-most populous, with an estimated 7 million inhabitants in 2020.

About two-thirds of these inhabitants live in the metropolitan area of Cape Town, which is also the provincial capital. The Western Cape was created in 1994 from part of the former Cape Province. It is a popular international tourist destination, known for its beaches, vineyards, mountains, and diverse wildlife.

Gauteng Province

The economic hub of South Africa, featuring vibrant cities and historical significance

About Gauteng

Gauteng, which means "place of gold", is the smallest province in South Africa but the most populous and economically significant. It is the wealthiest province in South Africa and is considered the financial hub of not only South Africa but the entire African continent.

Home to the cities of Johannesburg and Pretoria, Gauteng is a province of contrasts - from the bustling metropolis of Johannesburg to the more administrative capital city of Pretoria. Despite its small size, it contributes over 33% to South Africa's GDP.

KwaZulu-Natal Province

Where majestic mountains meet golden beaches, rich Zulu culture, and wildlife wonders

About KwaZulu-Natal

KwaZulu-Natal is a province of South Africa that was created in 1994 when the Zulu bantustan of KwaZulu and Natal Province were merged. It is located in the southeast of the country, enjoying a long shoreline beside the Indian Ocean and sharing borders with three other provinces and the countries of Mozambique, Eswatini and Lesotho.

Its capital is Pietermaritzburg, and its largest city is Durban. It is the second-most populous province in South Africa, with slightly fewer residents than Gauteng. The province is known for its beautiful beaches, the Drakensberg mountains, and its rich Zulu heritage.

Eastern Cape Province

Home to pristine Nature's Valley, the Wild Coast, and Nelson Mandela's birthplace

About Eastern Cape

The Eastern Cape is a province of South Africa. Its capital is Bhisho, but its two largest cities are East London and Gqeberha. The Eastern Cape is the second largest of the nine provinces and was formed in 1994 out of the Xhosa homelands of Transkei and Ciskei, together with the eastern portion of the Cape Province.

The province is known for its natural beauty, including the rugged and unspoiled Wild Coast, the mountainous southern Drakensberg region, and the beaches of Nature's Valley. It is also the birthplace of many prominent South Africans, including Nelson Mandela, Thabo Mbeki, and Steve Biko.

KwaZulu-Natal Free State

Free State Province

Wide open spaces, stunning sandstone formations, and the judicial capital

About Free State

The Free State is a province of South Africa. Its capital is Bloemfontein, which is also South Africa's judicial capital. The Free State is situated on flat boundless plains in the heart of South Africa. The province is the agricultural hub of South Africa, with its fertile soil and well-developed infrastructure.

The Free State is particularly known for its contribution to South Africa's grain production and for the Golden Gate Highlands National Park with its stunning sandstone formations. The province also hosts the Vredefort Dome, the world's oldest and largest meteorite impact site.

Eastern Cape Limpopo

Limpopo Province

The Great North with exceptional wildlife viewing and ancient cultural sites

About Limpopo

Limpopo is the northernmost province of South Africa. It is named after the Limpopo River, which forms the province's western and northern borders. The capital and largest city in the province is Polokwane. Limpopo was formed from the northern region of Transvaal Province in 1994.

The province is famous for its wildlife and nature reserves, including the northern part of Kruger National Park, Mapungubwe National Park (a UNESCO World Heritage Site), and numerous private game reserves. Limpopo is also known for its baobab trees, ancient ruins, and rich cultural heritage.

Mpumalanga Province

Where spectacular landscapes meet incredible wildlife experiences

About Mpumalanga

Mpumalanga is a province in eastern South Africa, bordering the nations of Swaziland and Mozambique. It was formed in 1994, when the Eastern Transvaal was merged with the former bantustans KaNgwane, KwaNdebele and parts of Lebowa and Gazankulu. The capital is Mbombela.

Mpumalanga means "the place where the sun rises" and is known for its spectacular landscapes and game reserves. It is home to the southern part of Kruger National Park, the stunning Blyde River Canyon, and the famous Panorama Route. The province is a major tourist destination for both local and international visitors.

Limpopo North West

North West Province

Entertainment and wildlife combine in this popular tourist destination

About North West

The North West is a province of South Africa. Its capital is Mahikeng. The province is located to the west of the major population centre of Gauteng and south of Botswana. It was formed after the end of Apartheid in 1994, and includes parts of the former Transvaal Province and Cape Province.

The North West is known for Sun City and the Pilanesberg National Park, which are major tourist attractions. The province also features the Hartbeespoort Dam, a popular weekend destination for residents of Gauteng. The economy is dominated by mining, particularly platinum and gold.

Northern Cape Province

Vast semi-desert landscapes, spectacular star-gazing, and unique wildlife

About Northern Cape

The Northern Cape is the largest and most sparsely populated province of South Africa. It was created in 1994 when the Cape Province was split up. Its capital is Kimberley. The Northern Cape includes the Kalahari Gemsbok National Park, part of the Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park, and the Augrabies Falls.

The province is famous for its diamond mines, particularly the Big Hole in Kimberley. It is also known for its spectacular spring flowers in Namaqualand, the Kalahari desert, and excellent stargazing opportunities due to its clear skies and low light pollution. The Orange River flows through the province, providing irrigation for agriculture.

The Big hole

The Big Hole in Kimberley is the largest hand-dug excavation in the world, a historic diamond mine that shaped South Africa’s diamond industry and now serves as a major tourist attraction.
