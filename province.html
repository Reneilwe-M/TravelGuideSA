About Western Cape

The Western Cape is a province of South Africa, situated on the southwestern coast of the country. It is the fourth-largest of the nine provinces with an area of 129,449 square kilometres, and the third-most populous, with an estimated 7 million inhabitants in 2020.

About two-thirds of these inhabitants live in the metropolitan area of Cape Town, which is also the provincial capital. The Western Cape was created in 1994 from part of the former Cape Province. It is a popular international tourist destination, known for its beaches, vineyards, mountains, and diverse wildlife.

Top Attractions

Table Mountain

Iconic flat-topped mountain forming a dramatic backdrop to Cape Town. Take the cable car for breathtaking views.

Cape Winelands

World-renowned wine regions including Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Paarl with historic estates and tastings.

Cape Point

Dramatic headland at the meeting point of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans in Table Mountain National Park.

Robben Island

UNESCO World Heritage Site where Nelson Mandela was imprisoned for 18 years during apartheid.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

September to November for spring flowers, or March to May for pleasant weather.

Local Cuisine

Try bobotie, braai (barbecue), and fresh seafood. Don't miss the world-class wines.

Getting Around

Rent a car for flexibility. Uber is widely available in urban areas.

Currency

South African Rand (ZAR). Credit cards widely accepted.
